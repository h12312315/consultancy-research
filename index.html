Design consultancy on X — what the field looks like

15 design freelancers and studios pulled from your Following list, analyzed across who they are, their X strategy, services, and modeled revenue.

On revenue: none publish full financials, so every figure is a modeled estimate except two who stated their own — @basit_designs (~$25K MRR client work) and @danmall (coaching past $100K in 8 months).

01 — Key insights

Overview

Reach varies wildly

The 15 span 172K followers (Meng To) down to 1.8K (Ata) — yet the small accounts compete on engagement, not size.

Engagement > follower count

@hunterthompson (3.3K) and @theatashka (1.8K) pull 200K+ and 39K views per post — far above what their counts predict.

Proof-of-work wins

The best posts are screen recordings and carousels of real UI, motion and before/afters — not advice threads.

Hybrid is the high tier

Everyone clearing ~$300K+ sells a by-product of client work — assets, courses, or a tool — on top of services.

What this suggests for you

As a UIUX/product designer, the best risk-reward isn’t pure freelance or pure course-selling — it’s the studio-plus-productized hybrid. Client work funds the lights; a productized asset or course compounds the audience. @basit_designs and @marina_uiux are the cleanest models to study.

The on-ramp is consistent, visual, build-in-public posting at daily cadence, anchored to one repeatable niche (e.g., Framer/Webflow marketing sites for a specific vertical) rather than “I design everything.”

03 — Content strategy pattern

What actually works on the timeline
  • The currency is visual proof-of-work, not adviceEvidence@marcelkargul, @hunterthompson and @derrkdotcom lean almost entirely on screen-recorded client case studies and motion reels — their pinned posts are videos of real builds.
  • Cadence is high — daily or near-dailyEvidenceConsistency over years: @MengTo 28.3K posts, @danmall 27.6K, @marcelkargul 26.2K. The volume, not a one-off viral hit, builds the audience.
  • Engagement-per-follower is the real signalEvidence@hunterthompson (3.3K followers) hit 2.4K likes / 215K views on his pinned post; @theatashka (1.8K) hit 1.1K likes / 39K views.
  • Almost everyone threads an “AI design” angleEvidence@viktoroddy (Figma motion + AI sites), @MengTo (prompting landing pages with Gemini), @bbssppllvv (“make AI design not suck”).
  • The leverage move: productize a by-product of the workEvidenceGradients → @basit_designs (Grainient, 100K visits/mo); UI kits → @tranmautritam (Envato Power Elite); reference library → @bbssppllvv (Refero, 300K+ users); courses → @marina_uiux (10k+ students).

Three ways to build the studio

Structural options for a product + design consultancy, each with its own client-acquisition funnel. Built on the field research: the market is shifting to “design + build,” and the durable players run a land-and-expand motion.

How to use this: pick the model that matches your talent today, then read its funnel as the go-to-market plan. Most studios start at ② and grow toward ③.

01 — The three models

From loosest to most integrated
A

Two studios, cross-sell

Lightest to start · 2 brands

A separate product studio and design studio that win clients independently, then package a joint solution when a deal needs both.

Strengths
  • Two independent lead sources
  • Each can own a sharp niche/audience
  • Low bundling risk; test what gains traction
  • Cross-sell upside when demand appears
Trade-offs
  • Double brand + marketing overhead
  • Split focus across two engines
  • Coordination friction on joint deals
  • Harder to build one strong reputation
Best when: you have distinct product and design talent (or a partner) and want to keep optionality before committing to one brand.
Success cases: Pentagram (@pentagram) · ustwo (@ustwo) · after.design (@aliszu) — federated/specialist studios that stay narrow and team up for joint work.
B

Unified product + design studio

One brand · balanced

A single studio selling product strategy and design together — the default modern positioning buyers now expect.

Strengths
  • One strong brand and funnel to compound
  • Bundled projects = higher average value
  • Clearest story; matches how buyers shop
  • Simpler ops than a build team
Trade-offs
  • Must credibly staff both disciplines
  • “Jack of all trades” risk without a niche
  • You carry both crafts’ delivery load
Best when: you can deliver discovery → design end-to-end and want one clear positioning. The pragmatic starting point for most.
Success cases: MetaLab (@metalab) · Clay · Budarina Studio (@marina_uiux) — one brand selling product strategy + design together.
C

Product + design + front-end (E2E)

Heaviest · highest ticket

Own the whole outcome: strategy → design → shipped front-end. “We don’t just design it, we ship it.”

Strengths
  • Highest project value; stickiest retainers
  • Owns the result — strong accountability
  • Defensible; matches the market trend
  • 11 of 15 top firms (Report ②) are tri-disciplinary
Trade-offs
  • Must add/partner front-end engineering
  • Ops + utilization complexity
  • Slower to launch; higher fixed cost
Best when: you can add front-end capacity and want premium, end-to-end engagements that convert naturally into retainers.
Success cases: thoughtbot (@thoughtbot) · Work & Co · Kinetic Studio (@hunterthompson) — strategy → design → shipped front-end under one roof.

04 — How to choose

Match the model to your talent, then grow
  • Start where your talent already isDesign-strong solo → B. Have a front-end partner → C. Two distinct teams/audiences (product & design) → A.
  • The market is moving to “design + build”Report ② showed 11 of 15 leading firms are tri-disciplinary. C is the destination even if you launch at B — design the brand so you can grow into it.
  • Lead with the wedge, live on the retainerUse immediate-goal projects (MVP, launch strategy, training) to land; convert the best clients into retainers for predictable revenue.
  • The front of the funnel is identical regardlessFrom Report ①: daily visual proof-of-work in one sharp niche is what fills the top of all three funnels. Pick the niche before the model.

A pragmatic path

Launch as Model B (unified product + design) with a sharp niche and a land-and-expand offer ladder. Add a front-end partner for select builds, and once that demand is consistent, formalize into Model C. Keep Model A in your back pocket only if you end up with two genuinely distinct audiences worth marketing separately.

This is structural and go-to-market guidance, not financial, legal, or tax advice.
